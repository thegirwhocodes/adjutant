# Persona — SGT Maya Chen

**Rank:** E-5 Sergeant
**Unit:** 82nd Airborne Division, Fort Bragg, NC
**Time in service:** 6 years
**Job:** Squad leader, infantry

## A day in her admin life

5:30 AM PT. 6:45 AM accountability formation. Training all morning. Lunch at the dining facility. Afternoon: PMCS the squad's vehicles, a Sharp briefing she has to attend, a counseling she has to give one of her soldiers (DA-4856 due before close of business), a CCIR roll-up to push to her platoon sergeant.

Sometime in there, her platoon sergeant tells her the company commander needs her at JRTC at Fort Polk for a 5-day mission rehearsal in two weeks. Also her sister is getting married June 3 in Atlanta — she's been meaning to file leave for two months and keeps pushing it.

So tonight, after PT and training, she has to:

1. Open DTS, file a TDY trip to Fort Polk, get the per-diem math right (she always gets one of the lines wrong and DTS kicks it back)
2. File a DA-31 for ten days of leave, route it through her squad leader → platoon sergeant → 1SG → company commander → S1
3. Write the DA-4856 counseling for SPC Garcia, who's been late for formation twice
4. Read AR 600-8-10 to remind herself how leave accrual works because she's not sure if the 10 days she wants will overlap with the holiday block leave

Each of those is 20–60 minutes of paperwork. Total: 2–3 hours of evening time, after a 12-hour day, that she'd rather spend on the phone with her family.

## What Adjutant does for SGT Chen

She opens the laptop in the barracks rec room. WiFi is being weird tonight (it usually is). Doesn't matter — Adjutant runs offline.

She holds the mic and says everything she'd say to her S1 if she could:

> *"I need ten days of leave starting June 3 to attend my sister's wedding in Atlanta. Emergency contact is my mom Maria Chen at 919-555-0144. Also TDY to Fort Polk July 14 for 5 days, JRTC mission rehearsal, home station Fort Bragg. And I need to write up a counseling for SPC Marcus Garcia for being late to formation twice this week — purpose is corrective."*

In 30 seconds she has:
- A signed-ready DA-31 with leave accrual cited (AR 600-8-10 ¶ 4-3)
- A DD-1351-2 with the right Leesville/Fort Polk per-diem rates and 75% travel-day math done (JTR Chapter 2)
- A DA-4856 for SPC Garcia with the right counseling type and AR 623-3 reference

She emails them to her S1, posts up the printed copies for chain-of-command signatures, and goes to call her mom.

That's the persona we built for. That's the time we give back.

## Why this persona for SCSP

- **Universal** — every soldier has been in this version of an evening. Judges who served will recognize it instantly.
- **Specific** — the demo voice query has real names, real dates, real units. Not "what is leave."
- **Unclassified** — every form, every regulation, every per-diem rate is public.
- **30-hour buildable** — three forms, one corpus, one persona, one demo.
- **Scales** — same architecture supports a UPL doing urinalysis, an XO doing property, a 1LT writing OERs. We picked the most-touched persona first.
